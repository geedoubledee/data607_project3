About the job
The Junior Data Scientist, as part of SRI International's Center for Innovation Strategy and Policy (CISP), having flexiblity to work a hybrid work schedule, will contribute to the design and execution of data analysis tasks to support of a diverse range of clients. Based out of our Arlington, VA office, CISP helps organizations, regions, and countries achieve economic and social impact through effective investments in science, technology, and innovation. Our team supports clients with innovation policy research, analysis, and evaluations on projects in the following areas

Tech-based Economic and Workforce Development
Innovation Ecosystem Assessment
Innovation Strategy and Training
Complex Program Evaluation
Science and Technology Program Support
Advanced Analytics in R&D Analysis

As a Junior Data Scientist, you will

Extract, clean, structure, and validate data for analysis.
Identify patterns, outliers, and areas of inconsistencies through relationships that exist in the data using statistical analysis.
Build models for automated data analysis and predictive insights
Produce written documentation for code and datasets.
Write analytic content for reports including methods, findings, and recommendations for technical & non-technical audiences.
Collaborate with other data scientists and CISP team members to propose solutions, reduce inefficiencies, and support ongoing activities.
1-2 years using R and/or Python for data science.
1-2 years working with data scientists to support data cleaning, modeling, and statistical analysis.
Experience with text data, including preprocessing, part-of-speech tagging, topic modeling, unsupervised clustering, and classification.
Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Data Science, or another computationally-intensive discipline required.
U.S. Citizenship required due to government contract requirements.
