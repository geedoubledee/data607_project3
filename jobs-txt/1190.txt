About the job
SparkCognition Government Systems (SGS) is the first full-spectrum artificial intelligence (AI) company devoted entirely to the government and national defense mission. By developing and operationalizing next-generation AI-powered solutions, SGS enables government organizations to meet the needs of their most pressing national security missions. Using technologies built in the United States, SGS advances government operations by analyzing complex data to inform and accelerate intelligent decisions, applying predictive and prescriptive analytics to improve logistics and readiness, deploying autonomy technology for unmanned systems, using natural language processing for large scale processing of unstructured data, and more.

About The Role

SGS is looking for a Junior Data Scientist to join our team dedicated to developing and integrating AI solutions that address critical national security initiatives. As a Junior Data Scientist, you will use your analytical and statistical skills to evaluate data for bias and suitability, prepare data for modeling, develop and evaluate models, and communicate results internally and with customers.

Responsibilities:

Partner with other Data Scientists to apply machine learning (ML) expertise, as well as develop and deliver novel customer solutions
Independently and effectively engage with external technical stakeholders and subject matter experts to understand and solve critical business problems through the application of cutting-edge AI
Design and deploy ML models for commercial and industrial applications, such as anomaly detection, prescriptive maintenance, and logistics
Collaborate with project teams through all phases of the data science process, from data exploration and processing, feature selection and engineering, model training and testing, and information synthesis
Maintain and apply deep understanding and application of all relevant SparkCognition products/technologies to support ongoing projects and proposal work
Use and/or identify procedures and automated toolsets to efficiently and effectively perform data science activities
Contribute internally and externally to AI product development and key data science research areas

About You

Must be a U.S. Citizen and able to meet Security Clearance eligibility requirements for access to classified information
Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science, Statistics, Physics, Mathematics, Engineering, or a related scientific discipline
Strong knowledge and understanding of data science, including basic elements of machine learning, statistics, and probability
Strong understanding of descriptive and inferential statistics (e.g., Empirical Rule and hypothesis testing)
Experience with one or more data science programming languages (e.g., Python or R)
Experience with one or more ML frameworks (e.g., PyTorch, TensorFlow/Keras, and Theano)
Applied knowledge of ML techniques/algorithms including linear and logistic regression, neural networks, decision trees, Bayesian techniques, clustering, and anomaly detection
Ability to work independently and collaboratively on a project team, as well as effectively prioritize workload to meet deadlines and work objectives
Strong written and verbal communication skills
Ability to translate complex technical topics to internal and external stakeholders
Ability to form strong working relationships with team members, cross-departmentally, and customers' technical teams
