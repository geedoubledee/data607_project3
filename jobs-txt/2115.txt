Who we are
Acacia Center for Justice (Acacia) is a new non-profit that seeks to deliver and advocate for meaningful and effective access to justice and freedom for immigrants at risk of detention or deportation. Acacia works in partnership with a nationwide network of accountable and independent organizations committed to the critical work of providing legal orientation and representation services to adults and children facing deportation in the detained and non-detained settings. As a key part of this work, Acacia is responsible for delivering high quality research, evaluation and performance monitoring, and data analytics.
Who you are
The Director of Data Systems and Analytics will be responsible for developing and implementing an integrated data management and system architecture across the suite of programs, involving: data engineering, development of a program-wide codebase, and maintenance of data analytic environments, leveraging machine learning processes for the treatment of big data sets, code review, and review of data analytics for the purpose of contractually mandated reporting. In partnership with the Director of Research and Evaluation as well as the Managing Director of Research Evaluation and Data Analytics (REDA), this role is responsible for strategic planning and employment of cutting-edge technologies applied to all aspects of data management in support of the overarching research agenda. In this role, they will provide supervision, support, and mentorship to REDA staff working on projects and initiatives across the organization.
The Director of Data Systems and Analytics will be primarily responsible for the following:
Data reporting leadership
Develop and manage immigration data analytics used to produce deliverables for mandatory reporting requirements across all programs
Collaborate on program monitoring, evaluation and research initiatives with other senior leadership on REDA, finance, and program teams
Support the development and implementation of a multi-year reporting, research and analytics agenda
Support annual planning and budget development, in partnership with other REDA leadership
Develop research proposals in support of fundraising, in partnership with other REDA leadership.
Preparation of data system architecture for analysis and reporting
Develop and oversee protocols for ensuring consistency in data science and analytics approaches, including code review, data integrity and security, and other quality control measures
Oversee all aspects of data management and analysis across initiatives, including development of data collection and analysis process and protocols as needed.
Data systems design and management
Oversee all aspects of data ingestion as well as enhancements of the cloud based, data analytics space used by the REDA team
Collaborate with IT staff, software designers, compliance officers and other key staff to develop strategies for maintaining the security of the data analytics space and optimizing the analytics capacities of Acacia’s program databases (which inhabit this space)
Develop and implement data modeling techniques that allow for the integrated analysis of big data sets and Acacia program data (entered on Acacia databases) to meet contractually mandated reporting requirements
Work with the Director of Research and Evaluation and the Managing Director of REDA to organize and update data outputs in conformity with reporting requirements, as well as other research and program evaluation goals.
Hiring, supervision, and mentorship
Build data science teams, including development of staffing plans, hiring, recruitment, and mentorship, while coordinating with other members of the REDA leadership team to ensure internal consistency and collaboration
Supervise the data science and analytics portfolio on the REDA team, including managing relationships with external consultants and partners
Supervise, support, and mentor an interdisciplinary team.
Internal collaboration and support
Partner with policy, program and communications staff to develop coordinated strategies that leverage data science tools to produce evidence-based messaging and materials.
What qualifications do you have
Required:
Commitment to using analysis to address and undo racism and inequity in the immigration legal system; lived experience preferred
Advanced graduate training, Ph.D., or equivalent and 6+ years of work experience, or equivalent applied experience over 8+ years
Relevant work, educational background, or lived experience as a person directly impacted by the immigration system
Project supervision experience (5+ years), which includes supervision of data science/analytics teams and demonstrated success in the accomplishment of project goals and management of timelines, scope, deliverables, and budget
Advanced data science proficiencies and data analysis skill sets
Exceptional written and oral communications skills, including the ability to translate and present technical processes to non-technical internal and external audiences
Fluency in at least one programming language (e.g., R, Python, SQL, C/C++, Java); ability to learn R and other programming languages as-needed while in the role
Working knowledge of the AWS data product ecosystem (e.g., S3, ECS, Athena) or similar cloud-based products
Knowledge of federal government standards for cybersecurity and data privacy, and/or experience with best practices in these areas including handling of personally-identifiable information
Working knowledge of Git and GitHub and dedication to using these tools to conduct peer code reviews and uphold coding standards
Experience in working with dashboards or embedded embeds, through the use of software such as Tableau or Power BI or through collaboration with a development team
In accordance with federal contracts, a National Crime Information Center (NCIC) check and an Electronic Questionnaire for Investigations Processing (e-QIP) security clearance are required for this position (for additional information: https://www.dcsa.mil/is/eqip/)
Proficiency with Zoom, Slack, Microsoft Office, Outlook, Adobe, SharePoint, VPN, Smartsheet access, and other software applications as the need arises
Physical presence in the U.S. required.
Preferred:
Experience in the fields of computer science, operations research, economics, quantitative sociology, computational social science, statistics, measurement, epidemiology, or a related field, or experience working on evaluation and measurement on federal contracts
Experience using research to manage or evaluate work on federal programs
Fluency in a language other than English spoken by people impacted by immigration enforcement in the United States
