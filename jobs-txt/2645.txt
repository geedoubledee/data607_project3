Data Engineer, Integrity Data Hub (IDH) - 100% Remote position
Background on NASWA
The National Association of State Workforce Agencies (NASWA) is the national organization representing all 50 state workforce agencies, D.C. and U.S. territories. These agencies deliver training, employment, career, and business services, in addition to administering the unemployment insurance (UI), veteran reemployment, and labor market information programs. NASWA provides policy expertise, shares promising state practices, and promotes state innovation and leadership in workforce development.
One of the projects managed by the NASWA UI Integrity Center is the development of the UI Integrity Data Hub (IDH). The Data Hub is an improper payment detection and analytics platform that allows participating states to submit and score UI claims data against data sources for cross state matching, suspicious data element detection, identity, and bank account verification. The Data Hub project team is seeking a full-time Data Engineer to provide data engineering support for the infrastructure for development of ongoing reporting functions, analysis, and modeling capabilities. This role is expected to include development, maintenance, and optimization of data architectures, complex data queries, data extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) initiatives, and provide possible assistance on data analysis and reporting.
Brief Overview of Position
The Data Engineer will be responsible for a hands on role expanding and optimizing data quality, data pipelines, and architecture, as well as optimizing data flow and collection in the IDH AWS ecosystem. The Data Engineer will support NASWA software developers, system architects, and data/program analysts on data initiatives. The Data Engineer will ensure optimal data quality and consistent and secure delivery throughout ongoing projects. They must be self-directed and comfortable supporting the data needs of multiple teams and products. The Data Engineer will work with IDH staff, the Project Manager, and other members of the project team to meet data-related objectives.
